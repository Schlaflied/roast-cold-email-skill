> 收集真实世界中**有效的**批评式求职邮件。每一封都让对方回复了。  
> Collecting real-world cold emails that actually worked — because they were right.

欢迎社区投稿。提交方式：开一个 issue，标题写「[博物馆投稿]」，贴上原文（公司名可打码，措辞请保留原文）。  
Community submissions welcome. Open an issue titled "[博物馆投稿]" and paste the original text. Redact the company name if needed — but keep the exact wording. The wording is the whole point.

## 中文馆

### LMS 公司款

**收件人背景**：某 LMS 公司 VP of Marketing  
**效果**：对方当天回复，要求进一步聊

> Subject：你们卖学习平台，但你们的 newsletter 里没有一个学习数据
>
> [收件人姓名]，
>
> 我订阅了 [公司名] 的 newsletter 三个月。
>
> 你们卖的是"让学习可量化"。但你们自己发出的内容里，没有一条 completion rate，没有一条 engagement 数据，没有一次 A/B 测试结果。
>
> 你们在用你们产品最反对的方式做内容营销。
>
> 我在做这件事。如果你想聊，我的日历在这里。

**解读**：指出公司自身行为和产品价值主张之间的矛盾。不是在说"你们不行"，是在说"你们有一个你们自己都能看见的 gap"。收件人不会生气，他会觉得你说得对。

### AI 转型款

**收件人背景**：某企业培训公司 Head of Learning Technology  
**效果**：两天后回复，约了 30 分钟通话

> Subject：JD 写了 AI transformation，但连 OAuth scope 都没配对过
>
> [收件人姓名]，
>
> 你们最新的 Learning Technology Specialist JD 里提到了 AI transformation、automation、intelligent workflows。
>
> 同一份 JD 里，技术要求是"熟悉 Microsoft Office"。
>
> 我不是在嘲笑这件事。我是说，你们知道这个 gap 在哪里，但没有人能帮你们填。
>
> 我可以。

**解读**：技术细节暴露了 gap 的具体位置。对方不会因为被指出矛盾而生气——他们通常比你更清楚这个矛盾存在，只是没有人敢说出来。

## English Wing

### EdTech款

**Recipient context**: Director of Content @ an EdTech platform selling "data-driven learning"  
**Result**: Reply within 4 hours

> Subject: You sell learning. Do you practice it?
>
> [Name],
>
> I've been reading your newsletter for two months.
>
> Your platform promises to make learning measurable. Your own content has no engagement metrics, no A/B test findings, no completion rates. Not once.
>
> I'm not here to criticize. I'm here because that gap is exactly the kind of thing I fix.
>
> Worth 20 minutes?

**Translation**: The subject line does the work. Everything after it is just proof you did your homework.

### Automation 款

**Recipient context**: VP of People @ a mid-size company that published an "AI-first HR strategy" blog post  
**Result**: Reply the next morning, forwarded to hiring manager

> Subject: This email was automated. Your onboarding wasn't.
>
> [Name],
>
> I automated the research that found your company. I automated the part where I checked your careers page. I automated this delivery.
>
> Your onboarding documentation is still a 47-page PDF from 2019. I checked.
>
> That gap between what you say and what new hires experience on day one — I've spent two years closing exactly that.

**Translation**: The subject line is the hook. The "I checked" is the proof. One sentence of credentials at the end is enough.

### LinkedIn Premium 款

**Recipient context**: Talent Acquisition Manager actively posting about "modern recruiting"  
**Result**: Got laughed at, then hired

> Subject: I get a 47% reply rate without LinkedIn Premium
>
> [Name],
>
> You're paying for InMail. I'm not.
>
> My last 17 cold emails had a 47% reply rate. Zero LinkedIn Premium. One Gmail burner and a willingness to say something true.
>
> You're building a recruiting function. I think you'd find my approach useful.

**Translation**: The number is real. The self-awareness is the closer. Works best when you're emailing someone whose literal job is getting replies.

## 投稿格式说明 / Submission Guidelines

收到过有效的批评式邮件？或者你自己发出去成功了的？来投稿。

**格式要求**：
- 保留收件人行业背景（不需要真名）
- 保留效果描述（回复了没有、大概多久）
- 保留原文措辞，公司名打码为 `[公司名]`，人名打码为 `[收件人姓名]`
- 不接受"我觉得这封邮件应该有效"——只收录真实发送并收到回复的案例

**Issue 标题格式**：`[博物馆投稿] 行业类型 + 风格描述`  
**例如**：`[博物馆投稿] 企业培训公司 技术gap款`
